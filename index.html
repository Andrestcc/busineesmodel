Cloud Computing and Business Model

Introduction: In the current digital era, e-commerce systems are not simply virtual storefronts, but complex ecosystems based on the cloud.

This project analyzes the critical architecture of a digital business, breaking down how **Business Objectives** dictate **System Functionalities**, and how these, in turn, define strict **Information Requirements** to operate with efficiency, scalability, and security.

Business Objectives

1 Increase Global Sales

  • Reach new international markets through 24/7 availability.
  • Reduce shopping cart abandonment rate by 15%.
  • Implement automated Cross-selling and Up-selling strategies.

2 Optimize Operational Efficiency

  • Automate order processing to reduce human error.
  • Real-time inventory synchronization across channels.
  • Reduce support costs through customer self-service.

3 Customer Loyalty

  • Personalize user experience based on browsing history.
  • Implement integrated loyalty and rewards programs.
  • Ensure a seamless post-sale experience (tracking/returns).

4 Data-Driven Decision Making

  • Real-time monitoring of sales and traffic KPIs.
  • Predictive analysis for seasonal stock management.
  • Advanced customer segmentation for targeted marketing.

5 Scalability and Security

  • Ensure 99.9% uptime during traffic peaks (Black Friday).
  • Strict compliance with data protection regulations (GDPR/LPDP).
  • Flexible architecture to integrate new payment gateways.

System Functionalities

Front-Office

Catalog Management

Advanced search, dynamic filtering, product visualization, and variant control (size/color).

Transactional

Checkout & Payments

Persistent cart, real-time tax/shipping calculation, gateway integration (Stripe/PayPal).

Back-Office

Order Management (OMS)

Status workflow (pending, processing, shipped), shipping label generation, and invoicing.

Identity

User Management

Authentication (SSO/Social Login), profile management, saved addresses, and order history.

Logistics

Inventory Control

Temporary stock reservations, low stock alerts, multi-warehouse synchronization.

Analytics

Dashboard & Reporting

Visualization of sales by period, top-selling products, and user behavior.

Systems Analysis

Table 4.2: Objectives, Functionalities, and Information Requirements

Business Objective System Functionality Information Requirements
Display goods Digital catalog Dynamic text and graphics.
Provide product information Product database Product descriptions, stock numbers, and inventory levels.
Personalize / Customize product Customer on-site tracking Site log for every customer visit; data mining capability to identify common paths.
Engage customers in conversations On-site blog / User forums User feedback and community building features.
Execute a transaction Shopping cart / Payment system Secure credit card clearing; multiple payment options.
Accumulate customer information Customer database Name, address, phone, email, and online registration.
Provide after-sale support Sales database Customer ID, product purchased, date, payment, and shipment date.
Coordinate marketing / advertising Ad server / Email server Site behavior log of prospects and customers linked to marketing displays.
Understand marketing effectiveness Site tracking and reporting system Number of unique visitors, pages visited, and products purchased, identified by marketing campaign.
Provide production and supplier links Inventory management system Product levels, supplier ID, contact, and order quantity data.

Conclusions

The alignment between strategic objectives and information architecture is the determining factor in the success of an E-commerce business. It is not enough to have the functionality; precise data capture is required to feed back into the business model.

In a Cloud environment, this structure allows resources to scale dynamically according to demand informed by data, optimizing costs and maximizing the end-user experience.
